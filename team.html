Leadership

The women behind the movement.

Meet the founders and directors building WOLA from the ground up.

AM
Alinda Mutabazi
Founder & Director, WOLA
Education

LL.M. in Human Rights & Social Justice, University of Connecticut. LL.B., University of Nottingham.

Research & Scholarship

Published author in the Journal of Student Research. Presented at the LSE British Conference of Undergraduate Research.

Advocacy & Leadership

Founder of WOLA, building a cross-continental movement equipping young African women with the tools, networks, and confidence to lead Africa's future from within.

Get in Touch
AA
Annie M. Adikin
Regional Director for Africa, WOLA
Leadership

First female President of the Makerere Law Society in over two decades. Former Deputy Editor-in-Chief, Makerere Law Journal; Vice President, Uganda Law Students Association (ULSA).

Law, Gender & Technology

Ugandan gender activist and legal researcher working at the intersection of law, technology, and governance. Published work focuses on digital justice, AI ethics, and the safety of women and girls online in African contexts.

Alternative Dispute Resolution

Specialist in international arbitration with a focus on how AI and digital assets are reshaping arbitral procedure, evidence, enforcement, and cross-border dispute resolution.
